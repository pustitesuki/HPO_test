The problem

Most AI in clinical review is a liability waiting to happen

Reviewers spend the majority of their time hunting for facts rather than applying clinical judgement. When a determination lands in appeals, the reasoning trail is thin or missing entirely.

Generic AI compounds the problem — confident outputs, invisible logic, no chain of custody from finding to evidence.

  • Determinations challenged on appeal because reasoning wasn't on the page
  • Reviewers spending 45+ minutes finding clinical facts per case
  • AI outputs that can't be traced to a source — unacceptable in regulated decisions
The engine behind every GTS solution

CORE — Clinical ontology and reasoning engine

CORE is not a chatbot wrapper. It is a physician-validated knowledge graph with deterministic reasoning on top. Every output is traceable to a guideline page, a clinical record line, or both.

01
Verified evidence base
Built on ACOEM, MTUS, and ODG — the same guidelines that govern UR and IMR decisions. Physician-curated and continuously updated.
02
Traceable reasoning
Every finding maps to a citation. Every citation maps to a page. The chain of custody from clinical record to determination is explicit and auditable.
03
Glass Box outputs
No black boxes. Outputs are structured, explained, and reviewable by a clinician. If an expert wouldn't sign off on the reasoning, the system doesn't surface it.

How it works

From record to defensible output in three steps

The same sequence runs whether a physician is reviewing in real time or a pipeline is running overnight at scale.

01
Ingest
Records arrive — PDFs, FHIR, HL7, scanned documents. CORE reads, normalizes, and anchors every clinical element to structured ontology.
02
Reason
Clinical agents map findings against controlling guidelines. Every inference is explicit. Every gap is flagged. Nothing is inferred without a citation.
03
Surface
Output goes to your reviewer, your EHR, or your data platform — structured, cited, and ready for the next step in your workflow.
